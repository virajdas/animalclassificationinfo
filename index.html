Archaebacteria

The roughest of the toughest.

Archaebacteria are tiny creatures. They are prokaryotes and are unicellular so they only have/are made up of one cell. Archaebacteria have a cell wall which allows them to live in harsh environments that almost no other type of organism can live in such as hydrothermal vents, hot springs, places with no oxygen, and even locations with an overly excessive amount of acidity. Archaebacteria find and consume nutrients through methods of absorption, non-photosynthetic, photophosphorylation, and even chemosynthesis. Basically they are both heterotroph and autotroph. Archaebacteria move in 2 different ways: cilia (using threads) and flagella (a long tail, may have two, makes the cell aware of direction). Archaebacteria reproduce through asexual methods such as binary fission, budding, and fragmentation. 

  • Methanothermus fervidus
  • Methanofollis formosanus

Eubacteria

They're everywhere, but they're not all bad!

Eubacteria are prokaryotes and are unicellular with a cell wall! Eubacteria are both heterotroph and autotroph because they consume through the means of absorption, photosynthesis, or chemosynthesis. It really just depends on the species. Eubacteria are very free creatures as they move around using flagella, though they do not have cilia. Eubacteria usually reproduce through asexual means such as binary fission. Eubacteria can be found anywhere! From the bottom of the ocean to the inside of your body, to the soil and specifically sunny areas. 

  • Staphylococcus aureus
  • Listeria monocytogenes

Protista

Yay, we have a complex cell!

Protista happen to be Eukaryotic and unicellular for the most part, but there are some exceptions. Protista are both autotrophs and heterotrophs because they receive energy through absorption, photosynthesis, or even ingestion. It depends on the species. Some protists don't move while others move using 2 different ways: cilia (using threads) and flagella (a long tail, may have two, makes the cell aware of direction). They reproduce asexually most of the time, but some specific species of protists reproduce sexually. They can be found in general places like lakes ponds. 

  • Euglena gracilis
  • Paramecium caudatum - Slipper Animalcule
  • Amoeba proteus

Fungi

The fungi are fun guys.

Fungi are Eukaryotic. They consist of both unicellular and multicellular species. Those in the kingdom Fungi are heterotrophs as they accumulate energy through absorption. They can also feed on decomposing matter. Mostly, Fungi don't move but their spores are carried happily by the wind. One way they do move is that their hyphae extend out. They can reproduce sexually or asexually using spores. They live in/on dead organisms and areas they find wet and humid. 

  • Volvariella gloiocephala
  • Mycena stipata - Clustered Pine Bonnet
  • Dacryopinax spathularia - Fan Shaped Jelly

Plantae

They blossom into beauty.

Plantae are Eukaryotes and species in the kingdom plantae are all multicellular. They are autotrophs as they create their own food through photosynthesis. Vascular plants take in water through their roots while non-vascular plants use their entire body. Most of the time, plants don't move (they do grow in the direction of the light), but some planets move in other ways such as the venus fly trap which snaps (which in kind of moving) at super fast speeds. Some plants can reproduce asexually using mitosis and others sexually. I really just depends on the species. Everywhere from jungles to your backyard, they can be found as long as it’s near water.

  • Adenophorus tripinnatifidus - Royal Kihifern
  • Acleisanthes chenopodioides - Goosefoot Moonpod
  • Boscia albitrunca - The Shepherd Tree

Animalia

It's you. It's me. It's awesome!

Those in the kingdom Animalia have Eukaryotic cells. Animals are multicellular. Every living thing that falls under kingdom Animalia is a heterotroph because they must ingest plants or other animals. Most animals are motile, allowing them move. Their movement methods vary greatly from from legs to fins and flippers, to wings. Animals from the kingdom animalia can reproduce both sextually (which is for most species) and asexually (not very common). You can find animals everywhere on Earth as long as they have access to food and water.

  • Hydropotes inermis - Water Deer
  • Chlidonias hybrida - Whiskered Tern
  • Simpsonichthys parallelus - Simpson Killfish
